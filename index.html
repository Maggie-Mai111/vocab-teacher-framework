The Vocabulary Teachers’ Competencies Framework

Welcome to the Vocabulary Teachers’ Competencies Framework

This website introduces and supports the Vocabulary Teachers’ Competencies (VTC) Framework—a research-informed model designed to guide language and literacy teachers in delivering effective vocabulary instruction. While vocabulary is essential for students' language development and academic success, many educators report feeling underprepared to teach it confidently. The VTC Framework addresses this gap by synthesizing the latest research into a clear structure of core knowledge, technical skills, and professional capabilities.

The paper describing the VTC can be found here: [LINK]

On this page you will find an overview of the framework, its conceptual foundations, and practical applications in teacher education and professional development. Whether you're a teacher educator, researcher, or classroom teacher, you'll find tools and insights here to support vocabulary teaching across diverse contexts.

The three domains of the Vocabulary Teachers’ Competencies (VTC)

Domain 1: Knowledge of Vocabulary Research, Pedagogy and Resources

1. Understands the statistical nature of language

Understands the statistical nature of language (e.g., frequency, grapheme-phoneme correspondences etc.).

2. Understands the structures of vocabulary

Understands the structures of vocabulary (e.g., morphology, phonology etc.).

3. Understands the characteristics of different types of vocabulary

Understands the characteristics of different types of vocabulary (e.g., academic, technical, colloquial, tiered vocabulary etc.).

4. Understands how to source, critically evaluate resources for vocabulary instruction

Understands how to source, critically evaluate resources for vocabulary instruction (e.g., vocabulary lists, textbooks, assessment tools, AI etc.).

5. Understands current research-supported vocabulary pedagogies

Understands current research-supported vocabulary pedagogies (e.g., Generative Vocabulary Instruction, Robust Vocabulary Instruction, Four Strands etc.).

6. Understands current theories of vocabulary learning and development

Understands current theories of vocabulary learning and development (e.g., language acquisition, repetition, retrieval, word consciousness etc.).

Domain 2: Vocabulary Analysis and Instructional Skills

1. Can identify morphological, orthographic, semantic and phonological structures

Can identify morphological, orthographic, semantic and phonological structures (e.g., identify phonemes, stress, part of speech, morphemes etc.).

2. Can identify high-utility instructional words in texts effectively

Can identify high-utility instructional words in texts effectively (e.g., considers frequency, tiers, student needs, role in comprehension, etc.) and teach them effectively.

3. Can identify and teach interrelated elements of word knowledge

Can identify and teach interrelated elements of word knowledge (e.g., phonics, interactions between spelling, sound, and meaning etc.).

4. Can identify typical and atypical vocabulary development

Can identify typical and atypical vocabulary development (e.g., signs of dyslexia, DLD etc.).

5. Can assess the vocabulary demands of texts and design instruction aligned with learner needs

Can assess the vocabulary demands of texts (e.g., complexity) and design instruction aligned with learner needs.

6. Can identify and teach the relationships between words

Can identify and teach the relationships between words (e.g., morphological and semantic word families, synonyms etc.).

Domain 3: Professional and Socio-cultural Capabilities

1. Develops a structured approach to vocabulary instruction

Develops a structured approach to vocabulary instruction (e.g., a systematic long-term curriculum etc.).

2. Develops teaching practice over time through engaging with new vocabulary research and instructional strategies

Develops teaching practice over time through engaging with new vocabulary research and instructional strategies (e.g., professional development courses, new technology).

3. Develops student interest in vocabulary, assesses their progress and provides feedback

Develops student interest in vocabulary, assesses their progress and provides feedback.

4. Develops collaborative partnerships with colleagues to integrate vocabulary instruction within rich cross-curricular content

Develops collaborative partnerships with colleagues to integrate vocabulary instruction within rich cross-curricular content (e.g., content area vocabulary).

5. Develops differentiated vocabulary instruction

Develops differentiated vocabulary instruction (e.g., for multilingual students, special educational needs students etc.).

6. Develops equity through inclusive vocabulary instruction

Develops equity through inclusive vocabulary instruction (e.g., leverages background knowledge, culturally responsive teaching etc.).
