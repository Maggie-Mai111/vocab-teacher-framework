Vocabulary Teacher Competencies (VTC) Framework Website

Welcome to the Vocabulary Teacher Competencies (VTC) Framework Website

This website introduces and supports the Vocabulary Teacher’s Competencies (VTC) Framework—a research-informed model designed to guide language and literacy teachers in delivering effective vocabulary instruction. While vocabulary is essential for students’ language development and academic success, many educators report feeling underprepared to teach it confidently. The VTC Framework addresses this gap by synthesizing the latest research into a clear structure of core knowledge, technical skills, and professional capabilities.

Our platform provides access to the framework, its conceptual foundations, and practical applications in teacher education and professional development. Whether you're a teacher educator, researcher, or classroom teacher, you’ll find tools and insights here to support vocabulary teaching across diverse contexts. We aim to foster deeper integration of vocabulary pedagogy into curriculum planning, teacher training, and cross-disciplinary instruction, ultimately contributing to improved literacy outcomes for all learners.

Domain 1: Knowledge of Vocabulary Research and Resources

1. Understands the statistical nature of language

e.g., frequency, grapheme-phoneme correspondences, context, language experience etc. inform vocabulary teaching

2. Understands the structures of vocabulary

e.g., morphology, phonology, spelling etc.

3. Understands the characteristics of different types of vocabulary

e.g., academic, technical, colloquial, tiered vocabulary etc.

4. Understands how to source and critically evaluate resources for vocabulary instruction

e.g., vocabulary lists, texts, textbooks, AI tools etc.

5. Understands vocabulary’s role within language and literacy development

e.g., its role in comprehension, interaction

6. Understands current theories of how vocabulary is learned and links this knowledge to vocabulary teaching

e.g., first and second language acquisition

Domain 2: Technical Skills in Vocabulary Analysis

1. Can identify accurately the morphological, orthographic, semantic and phonological structures for words

e.g., transcribe phonemes, identify stress, part of speech, morphemes etc.

2. Can identify instructional words in text effectively

e.g., considers frequency, tiers, student needs, role in instructional text etc.

3. Can identify and teach the different elements of knowing a word and technical correspondences

e.g., phonics, how spelling, sound, and meaning can interrelate etc.

4. Can identify typical and atypical vocabulary development

e.g., potential cases of dyslexia, DLD etc.

5. Can identify the vocabulary demands of texts

e.g., text complexity etc.

6. Can identify and teach the relationships between words

e.g., morphological and semantic word families, synonyms etc.

Domain 3: Professional and Socio-cultural Capabilities

1. Develops a structured approach to vocabulary instruction

e.g., a systematic long-term curriculum etc.

2. Develops teaching practice over time through engaging with new vocabulary research and instructional strategies

e.g., professional development courses, new technology

3. Develops student interest in vocabulary, assesses their progress and provides feedback

(to be added)

4. Develops collaborative partnerships with colleagues to integrate vocabulary instruction within rich cross-curricular content

e.g., content area vocabulary

5. Develops differentiated vocabulary instruction

e.g., for multilingual students, special educational needs students etc.

6. Develops equity through inclusive vocabulary instruction

e.g., leverages background knowledge, culturally responsive teaching etc.
